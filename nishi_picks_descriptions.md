## BROOKLYN

**Oxomoco** *(Greenpoint)*
Mexican restaurant in Greenpoint — seriously good food, worth the trip to Brooklyn.

**Bao & Pancakes** *(Brooklyn)*
Exactly what it sounds like and better than you'd expect. Go.

**L'Industrie Pizzeria** *(Williamsburg)*
Really really good pizza in Williamsburg — don't skip this one.

**Emmy Squared** *(Williamsburg)*
The Emmy Squared burger and garlic pesto bread are life-changing. So so good.

**Brooklyn Bridge Park** *(Brooklyn Heights)*
Walk over the bridge and end up here — the views of Manhattan are everything.

**DUMBO** *(Brooklyn)*
Most Instagrammed spot in NYC — the bridge framed between the buildings is iconic.

---

## CHINATOWN

**Wo Hop** *(Chinatown)*
Classic Chinatown institution — been there forever, always packed for a reason.

---

## LITTLE ITALY / LOWER EAST SIDE

**Eileen's Special Cheesecake** *(Little Italy)*
The cheesecake here is unreal. Get a slice, eat it on the street.

**Lombardi's** *(Little Italy)*
America's first pizza restaurant. The coal-fired pizza is worth the wait.

**Katz's Delicatessen** *(Lower East Side)*
New York staple — smoked meats, massive sandwiches. Very long line but totally worth it.

**Russ & Daughters** *(Lower East Side)*
Bagels, cream soda, smoked salmon — the full New York Jewish deli experience.

**Russ & Daughters Cafe** *(Lower East Side)*
The sit-down version — really nice, take your time here.

**Golden Diner** *(Lower East Side)*
Most famous pancakes in NYC right now. Instagram darling that actually delivers.

---

## SOHO / WEST VILLAGE / GREENWICH

**Shuka** *(SoHo)*
Mediterranean in SoHo — beautiful food, beautiful room.

**Via Carota** *(West Village)*
Best cacio e pepe in the city, full stop. Book ahead or wait in line — either way, go.

**Arthur and Sons** *(West Village)*
Get the spicy rigatoni AND the Spumoni dessert — both are non-negotiable.

**Joe's Pizza** *(West Village)*
Classic NY slice, the OG on Carmine Street. No debate, just eat it.

**Magnolia Bakery** *(West Village)*
The banana pudding here is legendary — get the biggest cup.

**Van Leeuwen Ice Cream** *(West Village)*
Best ice cream in NYC, the flavors are insane.

**Off the Wagon** *(West Village)*
Party bar — this whole street is full of college bars, great if you want a fun night out.

**Washington Square Park** *(Greenwich Village)*
The arch, the fountain, the performers — this park has its own energy. Just sit for a bit.

**Strand Bookstore** *(Union Square)*
18 miles of books — you can get lost here for hours. Always worth a browse.

---

## EAST VILLAGE

**Soothr** *(East Village)*
Really good Thai in the East Village — loved eating here.

---

## CHELSEA / HUDSON YARDS

**Shukette** *(Chelsea)*
Middle Eastern in Chelsea — great vibes and great food.

**Kame** *(Chelsea)*
Amazing ramen, I had the tonkotsu. Tiny, not many seats, worth it.

**Mercado Little Spain** *(Hudson Yards)*
Spanish food market by José Andrés — so much good stuff to try, go hungry.

**Chelsea Market** *(Chelsea)*
Food hall in a converted factory — walk through, taste things, it's a whole vibe.

**The High Line** *(Chelsea)*
Elevated park on old railway tracks — walk the whole mile, it's beautiful.

**Little Island** *(Chelsea)*
The cutest floating park on the Hudson — go on a sunny day.

**EDGE** *(Hudson Yards)*
Outdoor observation deck with glass floor. Worth it for the views — terrifying in the best way.

**The Vessel** *(Hudson Yards)*
That giant honeycomb structure — wild to look at up close.

---

## FLATIRON / MIDTOWN

**Thai Villa** *(Flatiron)*
Really good Thai in Flatiron — solid go-to.

**Bryant Park** *(Midtown)*
Little park behind the library — great for sitting and watching the city go by.

**Ravagh Persian Grill** *(Korea Town)*
Great Persian grill near K-Town — the kebabs are really good.

**Culture Espresso** *(Midtown)*
The chocolate chip cookies here are dangerous — you'll go back for more.

**Halal Guys** *(Midtown)*
Chicken over rice, extra garlic sauce. New York staple — you HAVE to try it.

**MoMA** *(Midtown)*
Modern art museum — even if you're not an art person, this one converts you.

**St. Patrick's Cathedral** *(Midtown)*
Stunning cathedral — Rockefeller Center is right next door, there's usually a market too.

**Rockefeller Center** *(Midtown)*
The Top of the Rock views are stunning. Come for the vibes, stay for the view.

**Times Square** *(Midtown)*
I mean... you kind of have to go. Also great for catching a Broadway show.

**Empire State Building** *(Midtown)*
Classic NYC — book tickets in advance or the line is brutal.

## HELL'S KITCHEN / MIDTOWN EAST

**OBAO** *(Hell's Kitchen)*
Great Asian spot — I love their khao suey, get it.

**Philippe Chow** *(Midtown East)*
High-end Chinese — treat yourself, it's worth it for a special night.

## UPPER EAST SIDE

**Xi'an Famous Foods** *(Upper East Side)*
Get the lamb cumin hand-slapped noodles. It's SO good, I still think about them.

**The Met** *(Upper East Side)*
I love the Met — go to the rooftop with your entry, the view of Central Park is stunning.

---

## UPPER WEST SIDE

**New Absolute Bagels** *(Manhattan Valley)*
Phenomenal bagels — classic New York, no frills, perfect.

**Mama's Too** *(Upper West Side)*
Viral chicken alla vodka sandwich — blew up on Instagram and totally deserved it.

**American Museum of Natural History** *(Upper West Side)*
Night at the Museum vibes — the dinosaur skeletons and the blue whale are unreal.

**Central Park** *(Central Park)*
Bethesda Fountain, Sheep Meadow, Belvedere Castle — rent a bike if you have time.

## HARLEM

**Sylvia's Restaurant** *(Harlem)*
Famous Black Southern food in Harlem — the fried chicken is iconic. Super famous place.

---

## FINANCIAL DISTRICT

**The Dead Rabbit** *(Financial District)*
Really good bar — classic New York cocktail spot, go for a drink.

**Stone Street** *(Financial District)*
A whole cobblestone street of bars — great for after-work drinks, very New York.

**9/11 Memorial** *(Financial District)*
Really moving and important to see. Go with space to feel it.

---

## GOVERNORS ISLAND

**Governor's Island** *(Governors Island)*
Day trip — ferry from FiDi, spectacular views of Manhattan and the Statue of Liberty. Go with friends!
